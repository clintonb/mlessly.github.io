Michelle Lessly

Higher Education and Policy Expert

My ability to empower students is the most rewarding aspect of my 10 years as a student affairs and higher education professional. In seeking opportunities to create highest quality educational experiences for students, staff, and faculty alike, I find I am at my best when working within policy structures and strategic initiatives.

I believe data tells a story and uncovers solutions to problems. Using both qualitative and quantitative methods, I use research to bridge the gap between theory and praxis. I examine the application of higher education policy in a digital learning context, specifically focusing on the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) and massive open online courses (MOOCs).

I am passionate about creating public policy through the lens of ethics, equity, accessibility, and social justice. I also strive to make connections between students, policy makers, and education practitioners.

Currently, I work at MIT in the Division of Student Life, hold a Masters of Education in Higher Education, and am a Doctoral Candidate in Law and Public Policy (DLP) at Northeastern University (anticipated June 2016).
